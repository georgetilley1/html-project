My Interest in Coding

While in my first year at Univeristy, I was given the option to take a module about mathematical investigation skills. Since all the alternatives sounded awful, I decided to go for it. This proved to be a great decision, as the module involved a basic introduction to coding, which I almost instantly came to like.
The coding that I learnt was heavily maths-based, and was always meant to be a tool to aid in my studies, e.g. if I was studying a probability distribution and wanted to find a certain probability density function value, instead of doing the horrible calculations by hand, I would open up my computer and type out the few lines of code in order to get the answer.
After finishing my degree, I was left with a desire to continue expanding my knowledge of coding. I guess I like both front-end and back-end development, but if you put a gun to my head I would likely pick back-end.
