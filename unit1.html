1.1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

In modern business, IT plays a very important role. It helps to drive innovation, and innovation is the way for businesses to make profits faster than ever. IT gives businesses the tools to solve complex problems, helps them make better decisions, improves marketing, improves customer support, and improves resource management. Put simply, IT is to business what steam was to the industrial revolution. [1]



1.1.2 Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully – what do you need to make projects successful? What tools? What skills?

The key to a successful digital project is the ability to utilise the right methods, skills, and tools. Obviously, there are numerous options available for businesses and the like to make use of, for example they could use kanban (improving speed and quality of delivery by increasing visibility of work in progress and limiting multi-tasking), agile (collaborating to iteratively deliver whatever works), scrum (enable a small, cross-functional self-managing team to delivery fast) etc. [2]
The kind of tools that a digital project might require depends on the project, and the methods selected for it. For example, if kanban methodology is being used, then trello could be a key tool to utilise this. Some common staples include Microsoft Office, might also be appropriate. On a separate note, the key skills required for a successful project depend entirely on the relevant role - the skills that a project manager needs might be quite different than that of a team member. However, some common skills include problem-solving, a degree of social media knowledge, attention to detail, good communication, and relationship building, among others. [3]

When it comes to planning a website, the first step should be deciding on a general plan, and utilising a relevant methodology to carry out said plan, for example utilising kanban planning in trello. 1.1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

When a digital project is in the works, there a multitude of risks that could potentially occur to scupper things. Risks that could occur include:

  • Ineffective or low impact change management;
  • Artistice direction being muddled by too many committee decisions;
  • Differeing departmental requirements;
  • Reallocated resources;
  • Personal capacity;
  • Unavailable inputs when required;
  • Lack of personnel and resources;
  • Data issues;
  • Finding new workflows and methods problematic.
This is by no means an exhaustive list, but are some of the most common problems that can arise when carrying out a digital project. [4]



1.1.5 Describe how you would go about selecting and using IT systems and software – how do you select the right kind of technology for the job?

When it comes to choosing the right software for a project, it’s important to grasp first why you need a certain software, and with this in mind set about creating a list of software requirements that your project will need, such as what you need the software to do, ease-of-use, technological preferences (e.g. Windows or Mac? Cloud or On-Base?), budgetary requirements, method of getting reports back, the scalability of the software, and any requirements that any potential vendor/client will have. Once this is complete, begn searching for a software package that will do what you want to do, and will fulfill the requirement syou have set. [5]

1.1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

In a nutshell, the GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) is a worldwide privacy law regarding personal data - such as banking information and IP addresses - that affects the way that businesses can collect, store and use this data. Businesses that are to be found in direct breach of the GDPR are slapped with big fines, thus any digital project must comply by mapping their data, determining what data they need to keep, ensuring that security measures are put in place to prevent or contain any data breaches that might occur, making sure that acquiring and processing personal data only happens with and individual’s explicit consent, and stablishijng procedures for the handling of personal data. [6]

1.3.1 Review the benefits and drawbacks of IT tools and systems used in terms of productivity and efficiency – could you have been more efficient when making your website? How? Could you have done it in teams?

When it comes to the usage of IT tools and systems in terms of productivity and efficiency, there are multiple benefits, such as making collaboration easier, increasing communication, simplifies progress tracking, makes data and work more secure, and makes working remote much easier. Conversely, there are some drawbacks to these tools, such as that they can be quite distracting, they minimise interpersonal skills, they occasionally require updates that can be disruptive to your work flow, you need to spend time and work to learn to use them, and they sometimes have a price tag. In regards to my website, I could perhaps have been more efficient in time management, but I certainly utilised various IT tools that made planning and working on my project less of a headache than it would otherwise have been. furthermore, I believe that while the overall efficiency would have been improved by working in teams, the final product would have likely been quite different in both style and content, and personally I think that my website benefits from being a one-man project.

1.3.4 Describe how you would go about testing digital solutions

Once a problem has been detected, and the necessary changes have been made to correct it, the first and most fundamental step is to go back to the website - or whatever software/terminal you're working in - and reload it to check if the problem has been solved, or if it still persists. When testing a digital solution, it is often useful to test if a solution works in different browsers (e.g. Chrome or Safari), on different operating systems (e.g. Windows or Mac), or on different devices (e.g. desktop, laptop or mobile). Alternatively, if a problem was to do with images/margins/background/etc. alignment on the page, when a solution has been found it could be useful change the size of the page to see if it still works.
